Legal Framework

How It Works for
Foreign Buyers

Morocco's property laws are clear, transparent, and foreigner-friendly. Foreign nationals can purchase property with freehold ownership rights, no residency requirement, and a transaction handled through a licensed Moroccan notaire and registered with the Land Registry.

✓

Full Ownership Rights

Foreigners can own residential property outright, under their own name, in Morocco's urban areas. Full freehold, notarised, Land Registry-registered title.

✓

No Residency Required

You do not need to live in Morocco to purchase. The process can be completed remotely through properly prepared power of attorney documents.

✓

Income Repatriation

When structured correctly through a convertible dirham account, rental income and sale proceeds can be transferred abroad through the formal exchange framework.

✓

Legal Right to Rent

Foreign property owners can rent their units on a short-term or long-term basis. Income must be declared to Moroccan tax authorities under standard rules.

€

Transaction Costs

Total purchase transaction costs typically cover notaire fees, registration, and taxes. We help you understand every cost before you commit.

%

Mortgage Guidance

Moroccan banks may finance non-resident buyers. We help you prepare the right dossier and compare available options.
