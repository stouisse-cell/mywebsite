Legal Framework

How It Works for
Foreign Buyers

Morocco's property laws are clear, transparent, and foreigner-friendly. Foreign nationals can purchase property with full freehold ownership rights, no special government approval, and no residency requirement. The entire transaction is handled through a licensed Moroccan notaire and registered with the Land Registry (ANCFCC).

✓
Full Ownership Rights

Foreigners can own residential property outright, under their own name, in Morocco's urban areas. No partner, no trustee, no special structure required. Full freehold, notarised, Land Registry-registered title.

✓
No Residency Required

You do not need to live in Morocco or obtain a visa to purchase. The entire process can be completed remotely via power of attorney. Rahim International can guide you to a trusted notaire and legal representative.

✓
Free Repatriation of Income

When financed in foreign currency through a convertible dirham account, Morocco's Office des Changes guarantees complete freedom to transfer rental income and sale proceeds back abroad.

✓
Legal Right to Rent

Foreign property owners can legally rent their units on a short-term or long-term basis. No rental restrictions for non-Moroccan buyers. Income must be declared to Moroccan tax authorities under standard revenus fonciers rules.

€
Transaction Costs

Total purchase transaction costs typically range 6–8% of purchase price — covering notaire fees, registration, and taxes. These are consistent and transparent. No hidden charges when buying through a notarised process.

🏦
Mortgage for Foreigners

Moroccan banks offer mortgages to non-residents, typically at 5.5–7.0% — higher than resident rates but competitive internationally. Many European buyers choose to fund in cash and benefit from full repatriation rights.

Step by Step

The Purchase Process

A clear, guided journey from first interest to confirmed ownership — we walk with you through every stage.

01
Choose Your Unit

Select unit type and size with Rahim International's guidance. We advise on location, yield potential, and resale value. Reserve with a deposit to secure your unit.

02
Open a Convertible Dirham Account

Open a convertible dirham account (compte en dirhams convertibles) at a Moroccan bank. This is the essential step that protects your right to repatriate rental income and sale proceeds abroad.

03
Notarial Process

Compromis de vente signed. The notaire verifies title, performs full legal due diligence, and manages escrow. Typical timeline is 30–60 days to completion. We can represent you via power of attorney if you cannot be present.

04
Land Registry Registration

Title registered at ANCFCC in your name. You receive your Titre Foncier — the definitive proof of legal ownership, one of the most secure forms of property title in North Africa.

05
Management Begins

Rahim International furnishes, photographs, lists, and begins receiving guests on your behalf. Rental income is reported monthly and transferred to your account. You own the asset — we run it.
