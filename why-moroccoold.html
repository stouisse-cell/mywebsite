The Foundation

Why Morocco Stands Apart
for International Capital

Before the city, the country. Morocco provides the legal protections, economic stability, and investor guarantees that make Kénitra's opportunity not just compelling — but accessible, transparent, and safeguarded for international buyers.

⚖️
Unconditional Foreign Ownership

Foreign nationals acquire residential property outright in their own name — no local partner, no special structure, no restrictions. Freehold title is notarised and registered with Morocco's national Land Registry (ANCFCC), providing the same legal protections available to Moroccan citizens.

💱
Guaranteed Capital Repatriation

For purchases financed through a convertible dirham account, Morocco's Office des Changes guarantees the full transfer of both rental income and sale proceeds to your home country. This is a statutory protection — not a policy subject to discretionary approval — and it applies to every qualifying Kénitra investment.

📈
Sovereign Stability, Consistent Growth

GDP growth of 3.2% in 2024, projected at 3.9% in 2025 (IMF). Inflation held at 0.9%. One of Africa's highest sovereign credit ratings. Morocco's macroeconomic environment is purpose-built for long-term capital preservation — a stable monetary framework that underpins consistent asset appreciation.

✈️
World Cup 2030 — A Nation Transformed

Morocco co-hosts the 2030 FIFA World Cup alongside Spain and Portugal, triggering one of the largest infrastructure investment programmes in the country's history. Rabat — primary host city and 20 minutes from Kénitra by TGV — is at the centre of this transformation. The uplift on surrounding property markets is already beginning.

🌍
Africa's Atlantic Gateway

Two hours from Madrid. Four from Paris. Morocco sits at the convergence of Europe, Sub-Saharan Africa, and the Gulf — and is emerging as the continent's leading logistics and manufacturing hub. Kénitra sits at the heart of that corridor, with deep-water port access, motorway connectivity, and Africa's first high-speed rail network.

💰
Entry Pricing That Still Reflects Yesterday

Premium residential property in Kénitra trades at approximately €980–1,100/m² — materially below Casablanca and Marrakech, and a fraction of comparable coastal assets in Lisbon, Madrid, or the French Riviera. 01
A €1.2 Billion Industrial Anchor

In July 2025, Stellantis inaugurated a €1.2 billion expansion of its Kénitra manufacturing facility — the largest single automotive investment in Morocco's history. Targeting 535,000 vehicles per year and generating over 3,100 direct jobs, this expansion anchors a supply chain ecosystem drawing manufacturers, logistics operators, and technology companies from across Europe and Asia. Industrial investment at this scale is historically among the most reliable predictors of residential property appreciation in a secondary city.

High-speed rail connectivity linking a secondary city directly to a national capital is a consistent, well-documented driver of residential and commercial property value appreciation across global markets.
